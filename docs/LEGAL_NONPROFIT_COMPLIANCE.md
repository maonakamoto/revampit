# RevampIT - Nonprofit Legal Compliance & Mission Statement

## Swiss Nonprofit Organization Status

RevampIT operates as a **Swiss nonprofit organization (gemeinnützige Organisation)** under Swiss law. This document outlines our legal compliance framework and mission alignment.

---

## Legal Requirements for Swiss Nonprofits

### Core Legal Principles

1. **No Profit Distribution**
   - ✅ RevampIT **does not distribute profits** to members, founders, or shareholders
   - ✅ All surplus funds are reinvested into the organization's mission
   - ✅ No dividends or profit-sharing schemes

2. **Public Benefit Purpose (Gemeinnütziger Zweck)**
   - ✅ Activities must serve the public good
   - ✅ Mission must be clearly defined and non-commercial
   - ✅ Benefits must be accessible to the general public

3. **Non-Commercial Activities**
   - ✅ Primary purpose is **not** profit generation
   - ✅ Commercial activities (if any) must be **secondary** and support the mission
   - ✅ Revenue from services must be reinvested, not distributed

4. **Transparency & Accountability**
   - ✅ Financial records must be transparent
   - ✅ Annual reports required
   - ✅ Clear governance structure

## RevampIT Mission Statement

### Primary Mission: Technology Redistribution & Open Source Advocacy

RevampIT's mission is to **enable free exchange of technology** between individuals and groups, promoting open-source hardware and software as the ideal form of human collaboration.

### Core Activities (Nonprofit-Compliant)

#### 1. Technology Redistribution
- **Free/Nonprofit Exchange**: Redistribute technology (computers, hardware, software) on a **nonprofit, free basis**
- **Items Redistributed**:
  - Old computers and computer parts
  - Old synthesizers and DJ controllers
  - Old hardware (computers, peripherals)
  - Old software (proprietary software given away for free)
  - Compute resources (servers, cloud infrastructure)
  - Memory resources (servers, storage)
  - Bicycles and furniture (supporting sustainable living)

#### 2. Open Source Advocacy
- **Goal**: Enable open-source hardware and software to compete fairly with proprietary solutions
- **Philosophy**: Open source is the ideal way for humans to collaborate
- **Vision**: As development becomes more decentralized, open source will exceed the capacities of centralized corporations
- **Global Scope**: Technological collaboration and exchange beyond national boundaries

#### 3. Subscription Exchange & Funding
- **Purpose**: Enable easy funding of subscriptions when needed
- **Model**: When proprietary software subscriptions are necessary, facilitate community funding
- **Goal**: Maintain open-source alternatives until they reach sufficient maturity
- **Transition Path**: Provide computers equipped with fully open-source AI and blockchain solutions

#### 4. Environmental Impact Measurement
- **CO2 Tracking**: Measure all electricity use (toasters, TVs, computers, AI, robotics, food production)
- **Individual Awareness**: Enable individuals to understand their true environmental impact
- **Upgrade Evaluation**: Rank technology upgrades by environmental impact
- **Sustainability Focus**: Promote environmentally conscious technology choices

#### 5. Education & Workshops
- Technical education on Linux, open-source software, hardware repair
- Workshops on sustainable technology practices
- Digital inclusion programs

## Risk Areas to Monitor

### ⚠️ Activities That Could Jeopardize Nonprofit Status

1. **Profit Distribution**
   - ❌ Paying dividends to members
   - ❌ Excessive compensation to founders
   - ❌ Profit-sharing schemes

2. **Commercial Activities Dominance**
   - ⚠️ If commercial activities become primary purpose
   - ⚠️ If nonprofit activities become secondary

3. **Private Benefit**
   - ❌ Activities that primarily benefit specific individuals
   - ❌ Exclusive access to services

4. **Lack of Public Benefit**
   - ❌ Activities that don't serve public good
   - ❌ Closed membership organizations

### ✅ Mitigation Strategies

- **Regular Review**: Annual review of activities and mission alignment
- **Financial Oversight**: Transparent financial reporting
- **Mission Focus**: All activities clearly tied to mission
- **Public Access**: Services accessible to general public
- **Documentation**: Clear documentation of nonprofit purpose
